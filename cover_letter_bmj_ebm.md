We submit the manuscript **"Al-Mizan: A Browser-Based Evidence Equipoise Monitor for Real-Time Assessment of Cumulative Meta-Analysis Maturity"** for consideration in *BMJ Evidence-Based Medicine*.

**The problem:** Clinical trial duplication wastes resources and exposes patients to interventions already proven effective or harmful. Despite cumulative meta-analyses showing clear effects, new trials continue to be registered and conducted. No practical tool exists for clinicians, ethics committees, or funders to monitor when evidence has reached equipoise-breaking maturity in real time.

**Our solution:** Al-Mizan is a zero-install, browser-based tool that integrates three complementary assessments — cumulative meta-analysis, trial sequential analysis (TSA with alpha-spending boundaries), and the fragility index — into a single traffic-light verdict system. The tool determines whether a body of evidence has reached sufficient maturity to declare one treatment superior, or whether genuine uncertainty (equipoise) remains.

**Demonstration:** We apply Al-Mizan to three landmark clinical questions:
1. **Corticosteroids in traumatic brain injury** — evidence of harm reached maturity after CRASH trial
2. **Tranexamic acid in surgical bleeding** — benefit established but additional evidence still accumulating
3. **Intensive glucose control in ICU** — harm signal emerged with NICE-SUGAR, now mature

**Relevance to BMJ EBM:** This tool directly supports the journal's mission of helping clinicians use evidence effectively. It transforms the abstract concept of "sufficient evidence" into a concrete, visual assessment that ethics committees and data safety monitoring boards can use to evaluate whether new trials on established questions are ethically justified.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere. The tool and all code are freely available.
